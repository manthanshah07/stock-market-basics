Understanding the Stock Market

The stock market connects companies with investors. Businesses use it to raise money, and investors use it to grow their wealth. Here's how it works:

Key Components

  • Shares: A share represents a fraction of ownership in a company.
  • Stock Exchanges: Platforms like NSE and BSE where shares are traded.
  • Investors: Individuals or institutions buying and selling shares.
  • Brokers: Facilitate the buying and selling of shares online or offline.

Why Do Prices Fluctuate?

Share prices change based on several factors, including:

  • Company performance
  • Market demand and supply
  • Global and domestic economic trends
  • Investor sentiment

Key Market Participants

The stock market brings together multiple players, such as:

  • Retail Investors: Individuals investing their personal funds.
  • Institutional Investors: Large organizations like mutual funds.
  • Regulators: Bodies like SEBI in India ensuring fair practices.
